	## 📊 Summary of Fraud Statistics by Bank
	 
	### 💰 Santander UK (The Only Bank with Specific Loss Data)
	Santander is the only institution that provides detailed, quantifiable figures on lost funds and trends.
	 
	*   **Total Lost to Male Customers:** Over **£22 million** in H1 2026.
	    *   Male Daily Average Loss: Around **£100,000 per day**.
	    *   Major Drivers of Loss: Investment scams (**£14 million**) and purchase scams.
	*   **Total Lost to Female Customers:** **£14 million** (same period).
	*   **High-Impact Scam:** "Hi Dad" Impersonation Scams reported £160,000 in losses.
	*   **Historical Loss Data:** In Q1 2025, customers lost over **£18.4 million**, with purchase scams accounting for half of all claims.
	 
	### 🛡️  BBVA (Focus: Deterrence and Reduction)
	BBVA does not provide a total figure for money stolen; instead, they focus on their preventative success metrics.
	 
	*   **Thwarted Attempts:** Security systems have thwarted up to **75%** of all fraud attempts.
	*   **Loss Reduction:** Overall fraud losses have decreased by nearly **40%** in recent years.
	*   **Threat Increase:** The volume of cyberattacks globally has grown by over **100%**.
	 
	### 🇪🇸 CaixaBank & Banco Sabadell (Focus: National Reporting/Lack of Disclosure)
	Both banks explicitly state that they do not publish total client loss amounts or localized theft statistics in their general reports. Their reporting focuses on national trends and risk management instead.
	 
	*   **CaixaBank:** Provides macro data for Spain, noting that banking fraud accounts for **40% of all cyber incidents** managed by the national institute (INCIBE).
	*   **Banco Sabadell:** Reports large-scale credit risks through their Non-Performing Asset (NPA) ratio dropping to 2.45%, rather than listing specific client losses.
