Treatment must be comprehensive, addressing the physical brain changes, the psychological triggers, and the social environment. No single medication or therapy will suffice.

*   **Trauma-Informed Care:** If the person has a history of trauma, therapy must be able to process that trauma without using alcohol as an anesthetic.
 
#### C. Social Support and Long-Term Recovery

*   **Support Groups:** Programs like AA (Alcoholics Anonymous) provide peer support, accountability, and a sense of belonging.
*   **Lifestyle Changes:** Building healthy habits, improving sleep hygiene, nutrition, and developing hobbies are crucial for long-term stability.
