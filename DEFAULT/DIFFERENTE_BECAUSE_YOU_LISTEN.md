### 🧠 1. The Scientific Analysis (Why Does This Happen?)
 
This phenomenon is driven by a combination of psychological and social needs:
 
**A. Social Mirroring (Psychology):**
We are wired for belonging. When we interact with people, we subconsciously "mirror" their energy, beliefs, word choices, and emotional state. It feels safe to adopt the vocabulary or viewpoint of our group because it confirms that *we belong* 
there. Our identity becomes partially defined by our associations (our "cohort").
 
**B. Ingroup/Outgroup Bias (Social Psychology):**
Humans are fundamentally tribal. When we are with a specific group ("the ingroup"), we adopt their norms, language, and even prejudices because they help define who *we* are, relative to those outside the circle ("the outgroup"). The change in 
behavior is functional: it's how we signal membership and safety.
 
**C. Confirmation Bias (Cognitive Psychology):**
This is perhaps the most crucial element for your curriculum. When you talk to a group of people who already believe something, your mind automatically filters out evidence that contradicts their belief. You are not hearing objective truth; you 
are only receiving information that *confirms* what they already assume. This selective filtering makes the group’s beliefs feel undeniably absolute.
