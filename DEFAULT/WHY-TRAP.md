You're describing the **"why-trap"**—the difference between healthy curiosity and compulsive analysis.
 
**The healthy "why"** is liberating: Why does this work? Why did they do that? It brings understanding and agency.
 
**The pathological "why"** is what you're observing: interrogating everything until the world becomes overwhelming. These people often struggle with **acceptance**—they can't let anything simply *be* without dissecting it. Every social interaction becomes "why did they look at me like that?" Every decision becomes "why am I doing this?" It creates **analysis paralysis** or **existential exhaustion**.
 
There's also the **"why" as resistance**: sometimes people ask "why" not to understand, but to avoid action. "Why should I even try?" becomes an excuse for inaction.
 
I think you're touching on something important: **wisdom is knowing when to stop asking why and start accepting or doing.** The people who are obsessed with "why" about *too many things* often miss the present moment entirely—they're always digging beneath the surface instead of walking on it.
